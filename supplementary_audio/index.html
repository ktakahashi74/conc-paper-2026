The listening page is organized as a short orientation followed by evidence clips for Consonance Search, Hereditary Adaptation, and Temporal Scaffold. There is no standalone audio track for the selection-pressure assay because that result is primarily about lifetime statistics rather than a stable replayable scene.

Methodology

Seeds are taken by ordinal index from the paper's shared seed lists — no cherry-picking. The orientation clips and the Consonance Search / Hereditary Adaptation evidence tracks are rendered from generated scenario scripts; the Temporal Scaffold monitor is rendered directly by the paper tool. All outputs are mono WAV at 48 kHz with no added reverb or mastering.

These audio demonstrations are qualitative corroboration only. Quantitative claims rest on the paper's simulation statistics, not on listener ratings collected for these tracks.

No separate public track is provided for the spectral-to-temporal bridge check, because that result is treated in the paper as an implementation-level mechanism check rather than a primary behavioural assay.
