Audio supplement provides qualitative corroboration of the reported quantitative results; it is not a behavioural listening study.

Processing note: most scenario tracks apply segment fade-in/out in post-processing; segment boundaries are also muted briefly to reduce overlap clicks. Specifically, showcase, controls, hereditary_adaptation, and hereditary_adaptation_controls are fade-processed; self_organized_polyphony and self_organized_polyphony_no_hill are not.

The listening page is organized as a short orientation followed by evidence clips for Consonance Search, Hereditary Adaptation, and Temporal Scaffold. There is no standalone audio track for the selection-pressure assay because that result is primarily about lifetime statistics rather than a stable replayable scene.

In short: all public listening tracks are renderer-based except the three temporal-scaffold files above, which are rendered directly through the paper binary for reproducibility with the E3 monitor implementation.

Landscape scoring and part of the agent mechanics come from the parent Conchordal codebase; the assay-specific experiment logic is implemented in this repository, and its findings will be fed back into later Conchordal releases.

Post-process behavior is track-specific: quicklistening and hereditary tracks use fixed segment fades (1.0 s and 0.2 s respectively), while polyphony tracks only rewrite metadata for segmentation and are rendered without segment-level fades.

Seeds are taken by ordinal index from the paper's shared seed lists — no cherry-picking. All outputs are mono WAV at 48 kHz with no added reverb or mastering.

Timbral settings differ by assay. Orientation, Consonance Search, and Hereditary Adaptation use the same harmonic timbre (6 partials, per-note ADSR 0.080/0.450/0.500/0.450, matching brightness and pitch-smooth settings). Temporal Scaffold uses sine one-shot pulses (48 independent voices, per-note ADSR 0.001/0.024/0.000/0.002).

These audio demonstrations are qualitative corroboration only. Quantitative claims rest on the paper's simulation statistics, not on listener ratings collected for these tracks.

No separate public track is provided for the spectral-to-temporal bridge check, because that result is treated in the paper as an implementation-level mechanism check rather than a primary behavioural assay.
